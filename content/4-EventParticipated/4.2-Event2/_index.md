# Event Report: "FCAJ Community Day"

### Event Objectives

- Share practical knowledge on various AWS services, their usage, and key features.
- Present real-world products and projects built by fellow interns and developers.
- Provide exposure to real deployment workflows, from idea to operation.
- Broaden understanding of cloud computing and its practical application in personal and team projects.

### List of Sharing Sessions

- **AWS Services Deep Dive**: Usage, Features, and Practical Applications
- **From Idea to Product**: Real Projects Built on AWS
- **Behind the Build**: Technology Choices and Deployment Process

### Key Highlights

#### AWS Services in Practice
- Overview of commonly used AWS services and how to select the right one for a given system requirement.
- Practical tips on configuring and applying these services efficiently in real development scenarios.

#### Product Showcases
- Teams presented projects they had built, covering the full journey from initial idea to deployment.
- Discussion of the technology stack chosen for each product and the reasoning behind those choices.

#### Real-World Development Experience
- Insights into the challenges faced while building and operating real products, not just theoretical exercises.
- Lessons learned from turning an idea into a working, deployed solution.

### Key Learnings

#### Understanding AWS Services
- Gained a clearer picture of the range of AWS services available and how to match them to specific project needs.
- Learned practical considerations for choosing between different services depending on system requirements.

#### Product Development Experience
- Learned from real experiences of building products end to end — from ideation, through implementation, to operation.
- Understood common pitfalls and practical decisions made during actual project execution.

#### Broader Cloud Perspective
- Expanded knowledge of cloud technology beyond individual services, seeing how they fit together in real systems.
- Gained direction for developing personal skills and future project ideas.

### Application to Work

- **Apply appropriate AWS service selection** to current and future personal or team projects based on actual requirements.
- **Reference real project workflows** shared by the speakers when planning the development process for upcoming products.
- **Continue building cloud knowledge** to support long-term skill development and personal project planning.

### Event Experience

Attending **"FCAJ Community Day"** once again was a valuable opportunity to learn directly from practical sharing sessions on AWS services and real product development. Key experiences included:

#### Practical Knowledge on AWS Services
- Gained a clearer understanding of the strengths and use cases of various AWS services, along with practical tips for applying them effectively.
- The sessions helped connect service-level knowledge with real system design decisions.

#### Learning from Real Products
- Hearing teams present their own projects — from the initial idea, through the development process, to the technologies applied — offered a grounded view of how real products come together.
- Seeing the full journey from concept to operation made the theoretical knowledge feel much more tangible.

#### Broader Direction for Growth
- The event reinforced how much there still is to explore in cloud computing, while also giving a clearer sense of direction for future personal projects.
- Networking with the presenting teams provided additional context and inspiration for approaching upcoming builds.

#### Key Takeaways
- Understanding AWS services deeply enough to choose the right one for the job is just as important as knowing how to use them.
- Real product development involves far more decision-making and iteration than following a tutorial — seeing that process firsthand was valuable.
- Continuous learning and exposure to real projects help shape a clearer roadmap for personal skill and project development.
